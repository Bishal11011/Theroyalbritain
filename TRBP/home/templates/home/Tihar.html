Tihar


Tihar is the five days celebration in Yama Panchak.


We couldn't figure out the actual day of Tihar Festival because the Nepal lunar calendar isn't published in advance.
It usually occurs in the Nepali Kartik month (October to November on the Solar Calendar) and lasts five days.

The Five days of Tihar


Kaag Tihar - Crow Pooja


Kukur Tihar - Dog Pooja


Gai Tihar or Laxmi Pooja – Cow or Goddess of Wealth Pooja


Goru Tihar, Govardhan Pooja, Maha Puja, (Aatma pooja) – Ox Pooja


Bhai Tika, Bhai Dooj - Bother and Sister Pooja


Crows, dogs, cows, Ox and Laxmi, the goddess of prosperity and fortune, will all be honored at this festival.
Tihar is also known as the festival of lights because many candles and festive lanterns will be lit.





First day - Kag Tihar (Crow Puja)

The crow, Yama's informant, is honored on the first day of the festival, Kaag Tihar.



Second day - Kukur Tihar

The second day, Kukur Tihar, is dedicated to the worship of dogs as Yama's messengers.

Third day – Gai (cow) Puja and Laxmi Puja

Gai Tihar and Laxmi Puja are conducted on the third day. In the morning, cows are offered prayers and nourishment,
and in the evening, Goddess Laxmi is offered special prayers and puja.



Forth day - Goru Tihar, Govardhan Pooja, Maha Puja, (Aatma pooja) – Ox Worship (Pooja)

The fourth day is dedicated to the plow animal, oxen, and the Newari community also celebrates Maha Puja.
Three different types of pujas are done on Govardhan puja Goru Tihar. We also do Govardhan Puja, which entails
creating a govardhan parbat hill out of cow dung. In Hindu tradition, cow dung is extremely important.
It was once utilized for everything from nightlight (Methane) to polishing traditional house mud floors.
The Newar community performs Maha Puja, also known as self-puja, on this night. It is done in order to purify our bodies.

Fifth Day: Bhai Tika

On the fifth day, sisters apply tika on their brothers' foreheads and bestow blessings. Sisters put "Tika of five colors" Paanch Rangi Tika -
Yellow, green, red, blue, and white on their brothers' foreheads to ensure long life and pray to Yamraja for her brother's long life and prosperity on this day.
